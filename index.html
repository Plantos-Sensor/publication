Our Mission

Plantos is a curated annual publication exploring the complex web that connects our plates to our planet. We illuminate the intersections of food, culture, agriculture, and technology, with a special focus on Austin's vibrant food landscape while drawing parallels to global food systems. Our mission is to tell compelling stories that trace food from soil to table, examining the human hands, technological innovations, and cultural traditions that shape our relationship with what we eat. Through thoughtfully designed features and in-depth reporting, we investigate pressing issues in food security, environmental sustainability, and agricultural innovation, while celebrating the joy of cooking, eating, and growing food. We believe that understanding the full story of our food—from the 1.26 billion people working in agriculture globally to the local farmers at Austin's markets—is essential to building a more sustainable and equitable food future. Plantos serves as a bridge between local food cultures and global agricultural systems, offering readers a deeper understanding of how their food choices connect to larger environmental, economic, and social issues.

Join us in building a publication that celebrates excellence in storytelling and visual narratives.
